Projects

In the summer of 2019, I was a Business Development - Partnerships Manager at CarGurus, developing digital automotive retailing products. I built a competitive landscape, using internal consumer finance data and qualitative customer interviews, and meeting with key internal stakeholders. Next, I developed a product with a set of prioritized needs, balancing the demands of our users and used car dealerships. I recognized we needed two products to satisfy the two distinct dealership segments, uncovering a new $28MM annual revenue opportunity. Lastly, I structured an A/B Vapor Test for both products to help CarGurus test my product hypothesis. Through this experience, I was able to solidify my product experience in an established company and see an emerging product through the ideation and testing stage.

In June 2017, I started a legal technology platform to connect independent attorneys and start-ups. I owned the product creation and customer outreach, scheduling up to 30 key stakeholder interviews with entrepreneurs and attorneys to determine their pain points seeking legal support as they have started their business. As a result of these interviews, I determined which customer needs are the most desired, most notably an hourly consultation service for legal advice, and established a $10K contract with third party developers to build product features for these needs.

In September 2018, I started at Chicago Booth to pursue my MBA.

At Booth I was part of the New Products and Services Lab, where I conducted design research and pitched an innovative insurance product offering to Nationwide. The 10-week consulting engagement consisted of conducting customer interviewing to determine their insurance needs, prioritizing those needs based on importance and market opportunity, and designing a new product for the company to pursue.

In my Machine Learning class at Chicago Booth, I worked with a blood testing center in Greece to save costs in testing potential thalassemia pateints. As the cost of a simple blood test is $3 compared to a thalassemia blood test of $300-$500, the project could help lower socioeconomic patients get access to testing. Using R, I build an ensemble model using h2O neural networks and random forests to successfully predict 75% of patients using the simple blood test. My team and I presented our findings to the blood testing center and remained engaged to help pursue our missing of more affordable testing.
